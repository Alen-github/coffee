Coffee

Espresso

The espresso, also known as a short black, is approximately 1 oz. of highly concentrated coffee. Although simple in appearance, it can be difficult to master.
Black Eye

A black eye coffee is a very simple drink. It’s a cup of normal drip coffee, either iced or hot, with a double shot of espresso. The name is said to have come from the way the coffee looks when you add the shots in, but we think the name comes from the fact that this drink feels like a punch to the face. This thing will seriously wake you up, and it’s obvious why
Americano

Americanos are popular breakfast drinks and thought to have originated during World War II. Soldiers would add water to their coffee to extend their rations farther. The water dilutes the espresso while still maintaining a high level of caffeine
Double Espresso

A double espresso is typically exactly what it sounds like: two shots of espresso. That means about two ounces of espresso and 128 milligrams of caffeine
Red Eye

The Red Eye coffee is a classic espresso drink containing drip-brew coffee, which makes the coffee stronger. Ordering a ‘Red Eye’ tells the barista that you’re more tired than normal and you’ll need more caffeine.
